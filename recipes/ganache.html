Chocolate Ganache


Description

Chocolate ganache — made from just chocolate and cream — is a glaze, frosting, filling, or sauce. Ganache was reportedly created in 1869 by Parisian playwright and confectioner Paul Siraudin, who is said to have named it after a popular Vaudevillian comedy called Les Ganaches.

Ingredients

  • Semi-sweet chocolate
  • Heavy cream
  • Rum (optional)

Steps

  1. Place roughly chopped chocolate in a bowl and bring heavy cream to a boil in a small saucepan.
  2. As soon as the cream reaches a boil, pour it over the chocolate and stir until smooth and shiny.
  3. If you're using rum, add it at the end.
